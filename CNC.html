Introduction
I believe manufacturing is one of the most significant applications of robotics. I've also always been fascinated by manufacturing processes that bring engineering processes to life. Working as a CNC machinist at the USC Baum Family Makerspace, I've had the privilege of operating and programming a plethora of CNC and manual machines. These experiences have engrained design for manufacturing, material selection, project management, and communication skills, which have permeated through my other engineering endeavors. Below are some of the projects I've completed for various reknown USC design teams.
  • Manual Machining
  • CNC Machining
  • 3D Printing
Manual Machining
Manual milling on Prototrack 3-axis mill. A coolant rig was improvised to preserve the tool and improve the finish during prolonged operations
Inboard backets for the famous USC Formula SAE Racing team. Machined entirely manually on a Prototrack 3-axis mill.
Practice hub nuts for the USC Formula SAE Racing team. This was done on a Prototrack 3-axis manual mill with retrofitted programmable axes. Excessive runout and vibration led to ugly finishes, so a Hass VF-2 was later used instead.
